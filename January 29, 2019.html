EB3 purification day 1:
- GOAL: purify 6X-His tagged EB3 using Ni-NTA resin, cleave off His tag by TEV protease, dialyze into Ni-NTA compatible buffer
- resuspension, lysis, and pelleting of cell debris performed as previously described
- lysate was incubated at 4C for 1hr with equilibrated Ni-NTA resin
- resin was washed with 1L of each of the following buffers: 
  1. His-binding: 50mM Tris, 10mM imidazole, 500mM NaCl, pH 8
  2. Wash buffer 1: 50mM Tris, 30mM imidazole, 500mM NaCl, pH 8
  3. Wash buffer 2: 50mM Tris, 30mM imidazole, 100mM NaCl, pH 8
- 2 rounds of elution were performed, ~20mL each, with JDP elution buffer (50mM Tris, 300mM imidazole, 300mM NaCl, pH 8)
- EB3 was present in both elutions at low concentration (see attached gel); high molecular weight bands present in elution fractions may be (a) contaminants and/or (b) dimeric EB3—as samples were not boiled prior to loading on the gel, it is possible that EB3 dimers were not fully dissociated/denatured
- 1M DTT was added to final concentration of 1mM, and 5 TEV aliquots were added to the total elution volume and allowed to incubate at RT for 4 hours with rocking
- using 10k MWCO snakeskin dialysis tubing, the entire elution volume was dialyzed into 4L of His-binding buffer and allowing to incubate at 4C overnight
