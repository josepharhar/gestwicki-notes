Gel analysis of wash and elution:
- prep samples for gel as follows
  1. 8 uL flow through + 4 uL 3X loading dye
  2. 40 uL His-binding wash + 20 uL 3X loading dye
  3. 40 uL wash #1 + 20 uL 3X loading dye
  4. 40 uL ADP wash + 20 uL 3X loading dye
  5. 40 uL wash #2 + 20 uL 3X loading dye
  6. 8 uL elution + 4 uL 3X loading dye
- load 5 uL of ladder, and up to 45 uL of each sample, run at 240V for ~20 min
- stain for 1 min in microwave
- rinse with water
- destain with shaking
- RESULTS: ADP wash seemed to strip off an observable amount of 70; however, the final elution did not seem very clean, with multiple high intensity lower mw bands—>for this reason, we decided to move forward with SEC to separate out those other proteins/degradation products

TEV His purification:
- see J protein purification protocol
- prepared 7 mL resin per sample, washed with ~200 mL water and 200 mL His binding buffer
- after incubating sample with resin, collect flowthrough
- wash with His-binding buffer and collect (I washed with ~100 mL; in the future, only needed to wash with ~40 mL)
- elute TEV from resin with elution buffer (~30-40 mL) and collect
- run each fraction on a gel (loaded 8 uL of each sample) to determine which fractions contain the protein of interest
- RESULTS: the majority of A2 was observable in the flowthrough and the first wash, with some still present in the remaining wash volumes

Preparing for SEC:
- samples need to be concentrated to run on SEC; I concentrated the flowthrough and the first His-binding wash using two 10K concentrators, spinning at 4000 for 5 min intervals until the total volume was approx 10 mL
- SEC 200 was selected because the j proteins dimerize (otherwise, separation of 46kD A2 and smaller ~17 kD proteins would be better on SEC 75)
- column was prepared by washing with water, followed by equilibration with a modified J protein buffer: 50 mM Tris, 300 mM NaCl, pH 8 (all buffers are filtered before ACTA)
