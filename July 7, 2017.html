SEC to purify J proteins:
- 5 mL sample line was prepared by syringe loading NaOH, followed by water
- approx 2.5 mL of sample was loaded onto the sample line, followed by approx 1 mL of sample buffer to push the sample forward in the line
- run SEC 200, flow rate 1 mL per minute
- RESULTS: 3 main peaks, one of which was the sharpest and the other two were broader (fractions B3-C4)

Gel to analyze SEC:
- for each of the above listed fractions, 5 uL of sample was combined with 2.5 uL 3X SDS sample buffer and run on a gel at 240 V for 20 min
- after destaining, it seems that most fractions were relatively clean and that the SEC did not strongly separate fractions of interest; therefore, it was decided that the remaining sample (~7-8 mL) would be frozen as is, instead of going through SEC

Freezing protein aliquots:
- for samples run through SEC, they were separated into 3 groups by the SEC peaks; these samples were then frozen in 1 mL aliquots
- for the concentrated protein from 7/6/17 that did not run on SEC, samples were frozen in 500 uL aliquots
