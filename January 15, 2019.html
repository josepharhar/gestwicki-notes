Preparing Rosetta glycerol stocks:
- GOAL: from the 3 test EB3 Rosetta cultures, prepare glycerol stocks that can be used to start large-scale cultures
- for each of the three cultures, combined 500uL of culture with 500uL of 60% glycerol and stored at -80C

Preparation of TB and flasks for large-scale expression:
- autoclaved a 250mL E-flask, 4L of TB (1L water, 47.6g TB, 4mL glycerol) in large flasks, and 500mL of TB for use in starter cultures

EB3 test expression:
- GOALS: test expression of EB3 from 3 freshly transformed single colonies
- to each of 3 autoclaved 125mL E-flasks, 50mL TB and 50uL 100mg/mL amp were added, as well as 1mL of each respective overnight culture
- cultures were incubated at 37C, 180RPM for ~3 hrs until OD600 reached 0.6 (except for culture #2, which never reached 0.6—see attached)
- upon reaching OD600 0.6, 1mL was removed from each culture, spun down at max speed for 1 min, and pellet was stored at -20C
- following sample removal, cultures were induced with 1mM IPTG by adding 50uL of 1M IPTG, and allowed to incubate at 37C, 180RPM for 4 hours (except for culture #2)
- after 4 hours, 1mL of culture was removed and prepared as described above; cultures were then cooled to 25C and allowed to grow at 25C, 180RPM overnight
- to test for induction, each sample was resuspended in 100uL 1X SDS sample buffer, boiled 5 min, centrifuged at max speed 5 min, and then loaded onto a gel (see attached)
- uninduced samples were loaded in duplicates, at 14uL and 8uL volumes, and induced samples were loaded in duplicates, at 8uL and 4uL volumes
- RESULTS: no major band appeared at the expected molecular weight (~30kDa), suggesting that EB3 was not overexpressed
