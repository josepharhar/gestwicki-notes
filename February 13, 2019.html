EB3 ELISA day 2:
- GOAL: test a gradient of EB3 and WT tau for interaction by ELISA; upon determination of optimal conditions, this will be repeated with a closer dose range and with V337M tau
- performed by Kelly Montgomery: BCA of freshly dialyzed WT tau, final concentration 234.8uM
- prepared 800uL of 50uM tau by combining 170.36uL of tau with 629.64uL binding buffer and performed 5 2-fold dilutions by combining 400uL tau with 400uL binding buffer (concentrations prepared: 50uM, 25uM, 12.5uM, 6.25uM, 3.125uM, 1.5625uM)
- 30uL of tau (at each of the 6 respective concentrations described above) was added to each well (either wells coated with EB3 or buffer alone); as a control, 30uL of binding buffer was also added to duplicate wells
- after addition of tau to plates, ELISA was performed as described in Meredith’s protocol (see attached), except for noted changes to protocol
- changes to protocol: primary antibody was used at 1:10000 dilution (?)
- upon addition of TMB substrate, plates were allowed to incubate ~15min in a drawer at RT until blue color was achieved (see attached)
- upon addition of 1M HCl, wells turned yellow, with darkest blue wells turning darkest yellow (see attached)
- RESULTS: since a full curve wasn’t established, we will need to increase the dose range of tau to lower concentrations; it seems that all tested EB3 coating concentrations were similar, so a single concentration within that range will be used for future experiments
