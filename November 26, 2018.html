Troubleshooting ATPase assay:
- GOAL: determine which reagent(s) are causing color saturation and loss of gradient in signal
- approaches to test signal for NaPi gradient (control):
  1. compared the signal +/- addition of sodium citrate; while a gradient was still measurable for NaPi + malachite green reagent, the signal was saturated/plateaued upon addition of sodium citrate
  2. prepared fresh 32% sodium citrate, and compared to Hao’s and Izzy’s stocks; all three stocks of sodium citrate saturated/plateaued the signal
  3. accidentally prepared 3.2% sodium citrate; this maintained the signal gradient, however the overall signal was lower than optimized conditions for the assay
  4. diluted NaPi in either JDP buffer, water, or malachite green buffer; none of these changed the saturation by 32% sodium citrate
- OVERALL RESULTS: while it seems that addition of sodium citrate is the cause of the problem (loss of linear signal gradient for a standard control), it does not seem that the particular batch of sodium citrate is the source of this result
